# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We want PaperQuay to be a practical, respectful, and technically focused project for researchers, students, developers, and contributors. We expect participants to communicate clearly, act in good faith, and keep discussions useful for improving the software.

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful and constructive.
- Focus criticism on code, design, reproducibility, and user impact.
- Provide enough context when reporting bugs or proposing changes.
- Respect maintainers' time by keeping issues and pull requests focused.
- Be patient with users who are new to open source, research tooling, or desktop development.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, insults, threats, or discriminatory language.
- Personal attacks instead of technical discussion.
- Publishing private information, API keys, local data, or private documents without permission.
- Spam, low-effort noise, or repeated off-topic comments.
- Misrepresenting unofficial forks or builds as the official PaperQuay project.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit, hide, or delete comments; close issues; reject pull requests; or restrict participation when behavior harms the project or its community.

If you need to report a conduct issue, contact the maintainer privately through GitHub.
